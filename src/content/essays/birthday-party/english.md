One week before my birthday, I start discussing my birthday celebrations with my parents. Since my birthday is in winter, we usually celebrate at an indoor venue, while my brother's summer birthday is celebrated in outdoor recreational areas like parks. We invite our friends, and they give us lovely gifts, for which we give them a memento in return.

Since we celebrate my birthday indoors, every year, we choose a new venue such as a bouncing house or a dashing car ride. One year, we even had my birthday at an indoor football court, and we all played football together.

On the other hand, my younger brother's birthdays are always celebrated in open parks, where there is no time restriction. We have a great time playing games, eating food, and enjoying balloons. Indoor venues have a limited time frame, but this restriction does not apply outdoors.

We buy a new type or shape of cake every year. I enjoy ordering car, Pokémon, and superhero-shaped cakes. Choosing a new shape is always exciting for me, and I love visiting the bakery to find the perfect one. The taste is just as important as the shape, and I always choose a mango-flavored cake.

On both of our birthdays, my father donates money to feed orphaned children in India. Hundreds of children enjoy a good feast on our birthdays, and my father shows us pictures they send. I feel happy that they are fed on my birthday.

When I reached my teenage years, I stopped celebrating my birthday. However, I asked my dad to continue the tradition of feeding the children.
